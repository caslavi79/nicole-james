## Why the copy changes the way it does

A few structural notes for anyone reviewing:

**The H1 uses "Condo Curator."** This is Nicole's own LinkedIn positioning. It's already how she describes her work. Using it on the site aligns her Google/AI entity footprint (site, LinkedIn, @properties bio) around one phrase rather than three competing ones. From an AI SEO standpoint this compounds over time: every mention of "Condo Curator" next to "downtown Austin" tightens the entity association.

**Every paragraph carries at least one concrete fact** (a year, a number, a building, an address, a credential) per § 4.5's specificity rule. Paragraphs with no verifiable detail were cut or rewritten.

**The tone avoids the parallel rhetorical structures** the design team's draft used ("one agent, one market"; "most agents work the whole city, Nicole works fourteen buildings"). § 4.5 flags those as AI tells. The replacement copy uses declarative sentences of varied length.

**Testimonials are not written.** Fabricating testimonials is a real liability (and obvious to anyone who reads the site in Nicole's voice and then "her clients'" voices back-to-back). Real quotes, sourced from actual clients or existing reviews, are required before this section ships.

**The "fourteen towers" claim is retired.** There are more than fourteen named condo towers in downtown Austin, and committing to a specific count creates problems (does Nicole cover Tower X or not? the site says fourteen). The revised copy describes her focus as "downtown Austin condominium towers" without quantifying — accurate, defensible, and still specific.
