Countless buildings.
One specialty.

A constantly growing supply of condominium towers across downtown Austin. One agent who has worked them all. Floor by floor, HOA by HOA, cycle by cycle.

The practice is building-deep, not market-wide. Each downtown tower is its own micro-market. Bridges on The Park’s lake-view tier prices differently from its courtyard tier. The Independent’s east face from its west. The Austonian’s lower stack from its upper bands.

When a unit comes up at Fifth & West, 70 Rainey, or Four Seasons Residences, the comps, the HOA history, and the likely buyer are already known. The real diligence on a downtown condo is the building, not the listing. That’s where this practice starts.

§ 01 · For sellers

What I bring to a listing.

i

Staging and photography.

Prepared and shot to hold its own against new construction. The first showing happens on a screen.

ii

Christie’s buyer network.

Global luxury reach beyond the local MLS: relocators, second-home buyers, affiliate-network agents.

iii

Comps from inside the building.

Pricing drawn from your stack, your line, your view tier. Not a half-mile radius around the front door.

§ 02 · Off-market

Quiet conversations.

Discreet consultations and pre-market matching, before anything reaches the public market. The right introduction, quietly arranged.

A meaningful share of downtown’s best units transact without a sign, a listing, or an open house.

§ 03 · For buyers

All of it, working for you.

Building knowledge, the off-market network, and Christie’s reach work the other direction too. The shortlist starts with towers that actually fit. Showings cover the public market, the units being quietly shopped, and occasionally the ones an owner hasn’t decided to sell yet.

Diligence is where this earns its keep. HOA financials read line by line. Floor-line analysis on which stacks catch the capitol view corridor and which lose afternoon sun to the next tower over. Negotiation anchored to the building’s actual closed comps.
