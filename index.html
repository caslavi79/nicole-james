§ 01 · About Nicole

Building-level
knowledge.
Personal
accountability.

Nicole James’s practice is built on a simple premise: the right building changes everything. Rather than covering downtown Austin broadly, she has worked inside a select group of condominium towers since 2007, developing a level of familiarity that goes beyond comps and square footage. A downtown resident herself and a University of Houston graduate, she is a Christie’s International Real Estate Luxury Specialist, works out of @properties Lone Star, and is a member of the Downtown Austin Neighborhood Association.

At Bridges on The Park alone, she has represented more than ninety transactions, with the most units sold privately, record-setting prices, and among the highest number of closed transactions in the building. The same pattern, scaled smaller, holds across over 30 other downtown towers. Today her role is less about volume and more about guidance, and for many clients she becomes the point of reference long before a property ever reaches the public market.

Downtown Austin condo
HOA fees, compared.

What residents in five different buildings actually pay each month, and what their fees cover.

HOA fees are the line item most downtown Austin condo buyers underweight in their budget. The brochure number and the lived-experience number are rarely the same. What follows is a look at monthly dues in five downtown buildings I know well, what those fees actually fund, and the questions I ask on every buyer’s behalf before offer.

Bridges on The Park. A mid-rise on the lake with a concierge, pool, and fitness room. HOA fees for a typical two-bedroom run roughly $700 to $900 per month. The building has been well managed on reserves, which is part of why resale has held the way it has.
